# Diaper Count Callout — Placement Guide
**Created:** 2026-06-29
**Status:** Ready for implementation
**Related:** `content/protocols/protocol-outline-v1.md`

---

## Purpose

Survey data confirmed supply anxiety is universal in week 1. The "6 wet diapers/day by day 5" metric is the most accessible, no-equipment way for a mom to assess whether milk is transferring adequately. Per product decision, this callout should appear in every week 1 module as ambient, repeating reassurance — not a dedicated module, but a persistent callout block.

---

## Standard callout text (use verbatim)

> **Quick check:** 6+ wet diapers/day by day 5 means milk is getting through. Before day 5, even 1–2 wet diapers per feeding attempt is normal — colostrum is small but mighty.

Do not paraphrase, split, or modify the wording except in the cases flagged below under "Modules requiring modification."

### Cross-path shared modules

The shared modules do not have explicit week assignments in the outline. The six shared modules are shown below with a week-1 relevance assessment and recommended callout placement. **Implementation decision required:** which shared modules surface in week 1 vs. later should be confirmed before placing the callout.

| Module | Week-1 relevance | Callout placement recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Postpartum body — what's happening to you | High (first-week physical changes are the primary context) | End of WHAT TO KNOW — "as your body recovers, this is the one daily feeding check worth tracking" |
| Partner support — what to actually ask for | High (partners need this in week 1) | End of WHAT TO KNOW — positions the wet diaper check as something a partner can help track |
| Pediatrician visits — what to expect weeks 1–6 | High (day-3 and day-5 weight checks are week 1) | In WHAT TO KNOW, adjacent to the weight-check content — the pediatrician and mom are watching the same metric |
| Safe sleep and feeding in the night | High (night feeds are week 1) | End of WHAT TO KNOW — "between night feeds, wet diaper count is the daily reassurance check" |
| When to see your OB vs. your IBCLC vs. the ER | High (3am escalation decisions are week 1) | In the preamble or WHAT TO KNOW section — callout defines the normal threshold so the escalation thresholds in the module make sense |
| Your mental health matters too | Medium (PPD can begin in week 1, but this module's topic is distinct) | Omit or make optional — the callout reads as out-of-place in a mental health module and could inadvertently increase rumination on output adequacy in a vulnerable mom |

## UI component vs. copy-paste recommendation

**Strong recommendation: implement as a reusable UI component.**

Rationale:

The callout appears in 12 path-specific week 1 modules plus up to 5 of the 6 shared modules — a minimum of 17 placements at v1.0, expanding further when weeks 7–12 are added. At this repetition level, copy-pasting the text block into each module file creates a maintenance problem: any future update to the wording (clinical guidance change, advisor feedback, A/B test result) requires finding and editing 17+ files.

A reusable component solves this directly:
- **Single source of truth** for wording — change it once, updates everywhere
- **Consistent visual styling** — renders identically across all modules without relying on individual content authors matching the format
- **Instrumentable** — the component can be tracked analytically (impressions, tap-to-expand rate, time-on-callout) in a way that inline copy cannot be without separate tagging
- **Version-controllable** — if the callout ever needs path-specific variants (e.g., for an EP mom, referencing pumped output rather than direct nursing), conditional props can carry that without duplicating the base component

**Suggested implementation:** A `<DiaperCountCallout />` component (or equivalent in your stack) with:
- Default text: the standard callout verbatim
- Optional `variant` prop if a path-specific variant is needed (e.g., `variant="ep"` for B-path modules where pumped output is the mom-side metric)
- Rendered as a visually distinct callout block (light background, left border accent, not inline prose)
- Placement: authors drop the component tag into the module MDX/content file at the marked anchor point — no text to copy

If a component system isn't in place yet and content is currently authored as static markdown, a second-best option is a **content include/shortcode** (`{{< diaper-count-callout >}}`) that resolves at build time — same single-source benefit, slightly less runtime flexibility.

**Do not copy-paste** the text into each file. The callout is designed to be ambient and persistent, which means it will outlast v1.0. It needs to be maintainable.
