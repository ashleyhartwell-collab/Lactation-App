# Latched — IBCLC Design Partner Invitation

*For sharing with your IBCLC and doula friends. Designed to be readable on a phone in under 3 minutes.*

---

## What I'm building

I'm building **Latched** — an app that helps new moms succeed in their first 6 weeks of breastfeeding. It pairs a personalized 6-week protocol (the mom picks her path: exclusive nursing, exclusive pumping, or combo) with feed and pump tracking, AI-powered flange sizing, and 24/7 AI chat support that's grounded in IBCLC-reviewed clinical content.

The mom pays $49 one-time. Nothing recurring.

## Why I'm building it

You already know the problem. The US has an 86% breastfeeding initiation rate that collapses to 39% by twelve months. The drop-off is concentrated in the first 6 weeks postpartum — exactly the window where moms have the least support and the most acute pain.

Hospital lactation visits, insurance-funded telehealth IBCLCs, and free Reddit/TikTok advice all fail differently. **No tool today lets a mom get personalized, IBCLC-grade guidance at 3am, asynchronously, between her IBCLC visits.** Latched fills that gap.

## Why I want you involved

I'm a solo founder with lived experience as a new mom. I am not a clinician. To build something that actually works — clinically credible, safe, and respected by the people who refer it — I need IBCLC partners involved from day one. You are who I trust to tell me when I'm wrong.

I'm specifically asking 5-10 IBCLCs and doulas to be design partners during Phase 0 (the next 4-6 weeks) and into the MVP build.

## What being a design partner means

**Time commitment:** 1-2 hours per month, mostly async, over the next 4-6 months.

**What you'd do:**
- Try the Lovable prototype as it evolves and give me honest reactions (~30 min, twice)
- Review a small set of protocol content modules and flag anything clinically off (~1 hr, twice)
- Tell me what you're seeing in your practice that I'm missing
- Once the MVP is live, refer the moms who you think could benefit and tell me what they say back to you

**What you would NOT be on the hook for:**
- Authoring clinical content yourself (that's the formal advisor's job)
- Promoting or marketing Latched outside your normal practice
- Anything that could conflict with your professional ethics, hospital policy, or IBCLC scope of practice
- A contractual time commitment — you can step away anytime

## What's in it for you

**Influence the product.** You shape how Latched works at the moment when changes are still cheap. The product you'd recommend in six months is the one you helped design.

**Early access for your patients.** Free Latched accounts for the first 50 patients you refer, so cost is never a barrier. You can recommend without thinking about whether the mom can pay.

**Recognition.** Your name, photo, and credentials on the "design partner" page of latched.com, with your consent.

**A simple referral mechanism.** Your patients land on a Latched page that says "Recommended by [your name], IBCLC at [your practice]." Trust transfers. Conversion is dramatically higher than cold ads.

**Visibility into your patients' progress** (eventually). A simple dashboard showing aggregate, anonymized metrics for the moms who used your referral code: how many signed up, how many completed week 1, how many graduated at week 6. No patient-level PHI. This is the loop that closes the post-discharge gap you can't close today.

**Possible advisory equity for one formal partner.** I'm planning to bring one IBCLC on as a formal clinical advisor with 0.25-0.5% equity (or hourly comp if preferred). If that's of interest, let's talk separately.

## What this is NOT

- This is **not** a job offer or a paid contracting gig (with the exception of the one formal advisor role).
- This is **not** a request to refer me to your hospital procurement department. Latched is a consumer product. Moms pay; hospitals don't.
- This is **not** a sales pitch for you to buy something or sign anything binding.
- This is **not** an MLM, an affiliate program, or anything where you earn commission. Your patient pays $49 to Latched; you don't see any of that money. The reason to refer is that your patients have better outcomes.
